Leonardo da Vinci

Painting is poetry that is seen rather than felt, and poetry is painting that is felt rather than seen


Italian Renaissance polymath known for his skills in painting, science, and invention. Famous for works like "The Last Supper" and his anatomical drawings


Vincent van Gogh

I dream my painting and I paint my dream


Dutch Post-Impressionist painter known for his expressive use of color and emotional depth. Despite a troubled life, his works, including "Sunflowers" and "Irises," are now among the most famous in the world.

Pablo Picasso

Every child is an artist. The problem is how to remain an artist once he grows up


Spanish painter, sculptor, and co-founder of Cubism. Known for his versatility across various styles, including Blue Period and Rose Period works


Claude Monet

Color is my day-long obsession, joy, and torment


French Impressionist painter known for his series of paintings depicting the same scene in different light and seasons. Famous for works like "Impression, Sunrise" and "Haystacks


back to top

Salvador Dalí

Have no fear of perfection - you’ll never reach it


Spanish Surrealist artist known for his bizarre and dreamlike imagery. Dalí's eccentric personality and distinctive mustache contributed to his iconic status in the art world.


Frida Kahlo

I paint self-portraits because I am so often alone, because I am the person I know best


Mexican artist known for her self-portraits and works inspired by Mexican culture and folklore. Her paintings often reflect her physical and emotional pain following a bus accident


back to top

Rembrandt van Rijn

Choose only one master - Nature.


Dutch Golden Age painter known for his mastery of light and shadow. Rembrandt's works include portraits, landscapes, and historical scenes


Andy Warhol

In the future, everyone will be famous for 15 minutes


American artist and leading figure in the Pop Art movement. Warhol's work often explored the relationship between celebrity culture, advertising, and consumerism

Edvard Munch

I was walking along a path with two friends - the sun was setting - suddenly the sky turned blood red - I paused, feeling exhausted, and leaned on the fence - there was blood and tongues of fire above the blue-black fjord and the city


Norwegian painter and printmaker known for his expressionist works that explore themes of anxiety, sickness, and death. "The Scream" remains one of the most iconic images in art history
